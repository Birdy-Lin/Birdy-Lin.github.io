About

I am a Sociology student at Wuhan University whose work sits at the intersection of computational social science, human-computer interaction, and LLM-based social simulation. I ask how technology reshapes what people can do—and how we can model those dynamics computationally.

I am also a builder: an AI product lead shipping production systems, a co-founder of an NVIDIA Inception-certified startup, an indie game writer with 200,000 words of narrative, and an engineer who delivers end-to-end. The best social science, I believe, is done with one hand on the theory and the other on the keyboard.

Research Interests

Computational Sociology

Text mining, topic modeling, and agent-based modeling to study social phenomena at scale.

LLM-based Social Simulation

LLM-driven agents with steerable personality traits to simulate social dynamics and sociotechnical futures.

Human-Computer Interaction

How AI-mediated tools reshape human capabilities, labor processes, and knowledge work.

AI Agent Engineering

Reliable, domain-specific AI agents for engineering automation and industrial applications.

From sociological imagination to computational implementation — bridging the gap between understanding society and engineering its digital twin.
